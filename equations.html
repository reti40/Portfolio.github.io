    • John Rahn | Basic Atonal Theory

      Tn(x) = x + n (mod 12)
      Pitch Transposition
      Tnp(x) = x + n
      Pitch Inversion
      TnpI(x) = -x + n
      Pitch-class Inversion
      TnI(x) = -x + n (mod 12)

      General Rules
      Every TnI is its own inverse. Every TnpI is its own inverse.

    • Allen Forte | The Structure of Atonal Music

      S/S' ∈ Kh(T,T') iff S ⊃⊂ T & S ⊃⊂ T'
      (S ∈ Kh(T) implies S ∈ K(T))

      Set-complex K
      S ⊂ T & S ⊂ T' iff T' ⊂ S' & T ⊂ S'

    • Joseph N. Straus | Contextual-Inversion Spaces

      [x, y, z] -- {I(xy)} -- [x, y, I(xy) z] = L
      [x, y, z] -- {I(zz)} -- [I(zz) x, I(zz) y, z] = L'
      [x, y, z] -- {I(xz)} -- [x, I(xz) y, z] = P
      [x, y, z] -- {I(yy)} -- [I(yy) x, y, I(yy) z] = P' (slide)
      [x, y, z] -- {I(yz)} -- [I(yz) x, y, z] = R
      [x, y, z] -- {I(xx)} -- [x, I(xx) y, I(xx) z] = R'
      I(nm) = n + m = y
      TyI = -x + y
