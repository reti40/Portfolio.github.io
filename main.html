Chris Zacharias



He started his musical education at Shepherd University, attending an Intro to Jazz Improvisation and Music Theory course taught under Dr. Kurtis Adams while studying Jazz Guitar under Joshua Walker. He later attended Berklee College of Music where he studied under Bob Pilkington with a focus on the jazz idiom, receiving a BM in Jazz Composition. Post-graduation, Chris has transitioned towards contemporary composition through the pursuit of self-study - integrating ideas of set theory, Messiaen’s harmonic and rhythmic ideas, the use of golden ratio, Fibonacci sequence, Tonnetz strip, and the utilization of jazz theory, under the umbrella of developmental ideas prevalent in 12 tone music. This shift in focus has led to performances from the Boston-based New Jazz Orchestra to the California-based Pacific Chamber Orchestra. He continues to compose for a variety of ensembles straddling the line between contemporary and jazz composition.


Fundamentally, composition is a pursuit of individuality and self-expression: a journey of self-reflection as to how we assimilate into our environment in both our local communities to the grander cultural landscape and one's willingness to accept or reject norms and traditions that exist. This alignment, or lack thereof, to tradition connects the music to both the audience and musicians, cultivating a shared experience and consequentially diversifying the cultural hegemony into something that's conducive to an acceptance of diverse ideas. In seeking a unique soundscape, I try to keep this in mind. Expanding my horizons to deepen my understanding of past compositional practice allows me to understand my position and if, and how, I am retreading ground that's already been paved by those who came before me.


Developing interest in both music and composition during his collegiate years, Chris' extracurricular activities focus on other aspects of life. His current interest is learning how to code, focusing on Python, HTML, CSS, and JavaScript programing languages, utilizing Flask for website development along with developing other programs to help synthesis certain procedural tasks during pre-composition. Alongside learning to code, he finds fulfillment in developing his artistic expression through painting abstract pieces where, like his compositions, he focuses on the blending of colors and textural ideas.
